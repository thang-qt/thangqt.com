The scene is nearly monochrome, but the shifts are enough to keep it alive. Greens drift into gray and back again. The water is the quietest part, yet it carries the most information.

A palette like this is a reminder that color can be subtle without being dull. When everything is close in value, you start to notice texture and rhythm. The eye rests more often, and the page feels slower.

If I translate that to a layout, I lean into soft backgrounds and readable contrast. The content does not need a loud frame. It only needs a calm field to live on.
