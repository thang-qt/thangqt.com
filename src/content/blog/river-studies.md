The river is never the same twice, yet it is always a river. That tension makes it an endless subject: steady, moving, familiar, and new. I like to think of it as a lesson in visual pacing.

There is a soft rhythm to reflections. They take the sky and break it into smaller statements, like a paragraph with careful line breaks. Nothing feels rushed, and yet the surface is alive.

When I design, I try to borrow that rhythm. A layout should offer moments of calm, then moments that sharpen attention. Space does not mean emptiness; it is the framing that lets the eye breathe.

This is why I like a gentle background. It does not command attention, but it keeps the atmosphere present. It is the quiet that holds everything together.
